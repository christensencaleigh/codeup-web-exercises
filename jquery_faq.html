Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Margaret Everson, Counselor to the Secretary, is exercising the delegated authority of the Director of the National Park Service.

How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: Approximately 20,000.

How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.

What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior. Directly overseeing its operation is the department's Assistant Secretary for Fish, Wildlife and Parks.

How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2019: 327,516,619.

What is the number one most visited national park in 2019?
Great Smoky Mountains National Park

WHat is the second most visited national park?
Grand Canyon National Park

What is the third most visited national park?
Rocky Mountain National Park

What is the fourth most visited national park?

What is the fifth most visited national park?

Yellowstone National Park

  • It is the first national park.
  • Home of Old Faithful, a world-famous geyser, erupts every 92 minutes on average.
  • Yellowstone is larger than the state of Rhode Island and Delaware combined.

Big Bend National Park

  • With 450 bird species, there are more bird species in Big Bend than any other U.S. National Park.
  • The stars at night really are big and bright because Big Bend is so remote. The Milky Way rises in Big Bend during sunrise.
  • This region gets its name because the Rio Grande turns in the area in, well, a big bend.

Rocky Mountain National Park

  • There are rocks 1.7 billion years old in the park, and they are also some of the oldest in the National Parks System.
  • The Continental Divide runs through the park
  • Rocky Mountain National Park has 156 lakes
